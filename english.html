English Learning and Teaching

Colours have been shown to greatly aid memorisation. Students can learn to associate different colours with different types of words. Care should be taken to make sure they are not just memorising the order of colours. To do this, it may be best to just use colours as a learning aid and gradually remove them from materials as students become used to the language.

Words can change their part of speech at times depending on the context in which they are used. I address this by using the most common type of word that they are. This is easier to address when using a computer, though it will take more work to change the colours of the words.

I suggest using coloured outline boxes around the words, or coloured backgrounds rather than colouring the words themselves. Colouring the words can make them hard to read (often with yellow) and coloured words are not common in the real world.

Here is a guide to using colours to represent different parts of speech.

Colour - Part of Speech Matrix/Crib

Part of Speech Colour
Question Words Blue
Nouns Red
Verbs Green
Adjectives Yellow
Adverbs Purple
Pronouns Pink
Prepositions Orange
Conjunctions Brown
Articles Grey
